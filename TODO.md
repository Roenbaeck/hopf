# Scope for future work

The theorems in the current paper are complete.  Natural extensions are:

- derive higher-jet compatibility conditions for arbitrary nonlinear paths
  whose normalized second-order tensor is a nonzero coupled-kernel element;
  the paper now obstructs every pure coupled kernel ray, including all
  cross-degree, two-tower, and `(1,1)` mixtures, but third- and higher-order
  metric corrections are not classified;
- classify all zero-curvature planes, rather than only the planes tangent to
  the certified tori;
- classify all background-mixed signs for `(sigma, sigma, tau)` on
  `S^2 x S^2`; the singular-subsphere reduction and explicit oblique
  obstruction already settle full nonnegative curvature on every
  repeated-top ray in all dimensions `m,n >= 2`;
- investigate whether singular-subsphere reduction can bootstrap other
  sharp curvature, rigidity, or moduli results from low-dimensional models;
- determine the optimal negative-curvature margin as a function of the
  normalized top three singular values; the paper now gives a fully explicit
  dimension-free lower bound, but does not claim its constant is optimal;
- classify all matrices and mixed planes for which the quadratic curvature
  coefficient vanishes before gauge normalization and determine their first
  nonzero order, extending the quartic background-mixed spectral-gap
  construction;
- determine the full isometry group for generic and exceptional parameters;
- treat potentials beyond bilinear degree-one spherical harmonics;
- study whether related fixed-submanifold mechanisms survive on products of
  other compact symmetric spaces.

These are possible follow-up projects, not missing steps in the present
proof.
