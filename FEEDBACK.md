The draft has a solid spine. The part that feels genuinely new is the non-equivariant `\ell=1` section; the equivariant main theorem is correct, but it is mostly a structural/seam-theoretic repackaging of Hsiang-Kleiner, so the paper is strongest when it sells: classification, explicit curvature mechanism, and the new analytic obstruction in the symmetry-free `\ell=1` case.

The two immediate issues I’d fix before showing this around are:

- The conformal-product curvature proposition has a proof mismatch. The statement at [hopf_seam.tex](/Users/lars/Documents/GitHub/hopf/hopf_seam.tex#L484) gives cross terms `X_1(u)X_1(v)` and `X_2(u)X_2(v)`, but the proof at [hopf_seam.tex](/Users/lars/Documents/GitHub/hopf/hopf_seam.tex#L499) actually derives the square terms `X_1(v)^2` and `X_2(u)^2`, then says “the complete expression is” the stated formula. As written, that proposition is not proved.
- The scope remark still overclaims the `\ell=1` result. At [hopf_seam.tex](/Users/lars/Documents/GitHub/hopf/hopf_seam.tex#L200) it says the conformal Hessian structure “forces `K_mix >= 0` with equality everywhere,” but the paper only proves vanishing at critical points / on the special tori plus a perturbative nonnegativity statement and a conjecture.
- The torus-unification discussion is a bit too sweeping for the conformal-product case at [hopf_seam.tex](/Users/lars/Documents/GitHub/hopf/hopf_seam.tex#L1996). There the obstruction is really maximum-principle first, torus/Gauss-Bonnet only as interpretation.

On “immediate additional results”: yes, I think there are at least three low-hanging corollaries already latent in the draft.

- The broadest one is already essentially proved in Lemma 6.6(iii): for any compact product `M_1 x M_2` and any separable product seam `s=f(x_1)g(x_2)`, the Hessian metric `h + \gamma \nabla^2 s` has a point where all mixed sectional curvatures vanish. That deserves extraction as its own corollary from [hopf_seam.tex](/Users/lars/Documents/GitHub/hopf/hopf_seam.tex#L1391).
- You can package the torus argument as a general reflection-symmetry theorem: if a seam metric is invariant under a simultaneous reflection `R x R` of the two spheres, then the fixed set `Fix(R x R) = S^1 x S^1` is totally geodesic, so by Gauss-Bonnet there is a tangent mixed plane with `K <= 0`. The ingredients are already in [hopf_seam.tex](/Users/lars/Documents/GitHub/hopf/hopf_seam.tex#L1668) and [hopf_seam.tex](/Users/lars/Documents/GitHub/hopf/hopf_seam.tex#L1996). This would extend the current `\ell=1` torus argument to any reflection-symmetric seam/rule, not just the specific Hessian `\ell=1` family.
- The remark on `\ell >= 2` can likely be upgraded from remark to theorem for pure product seams: the draft already says that any product harmonic `Y_\ell^m(x_1)Y_\ell^m(x_2)` has mixed-flat critical points, and in reflection-adapted cases also totally geodesic torus obstructions, at [hopf_seam.tex](/Users/lars/Documents/GitHub/hopf/hopf_seam.tex#L1845). That is stronger than the current narrative suggests.

So my short verdict is: promising paper, with the real punch in §7, but I would not circulate it until the conformal-product proposition is repaired and the `\ell=1` claims are trimmed to exactly what is proved. I also checked `validate_tex.py`; refs/labels are clean.
